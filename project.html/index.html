ISLAMIC EMPIRE

The Islamic caliphate spread over an area of 22 lakh square kilometers. The first caliph, Hazrat Abu Bakr, had a limited reign, but Hazrat Umar greatly expanded the influence of Islam and ruled over a significant portion of the world. After him, Hazrat Uthman and then Hazrat Ali governed this magnificent empire.

OTTOMAN EMPIRE

The Ottoman Empire was a powerful Islamic empire that emerged after the establishment of Turkish rule in the 13th century and lasted until the 20th century. It became a center of power in Asia, Africa, and Europe. The Ottoman Empire achieved significant accomplishments, including advancements in trade, science and technology, culture, and governance. They built many beautiful cities, established examples of mosques, and formed a strong military. During the Ottoman Empire, many historical events took place, which contributed to its fame as a prominent empire.

SELJOK EMPIRE

The Seljuk Empire was an important Islamic empire that ruled over the Middle East in the 11th and 12th centuries. It was of Turkic origin, and its caliphs were also Turks. The Seljuks achieved significant accomplishments and played a crucial role in the advancement of Islamic culture, education, and architecture. They built numerous great cities and established examples of mosques, schools, and palaces.
